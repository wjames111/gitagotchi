gitagotchi · day, night & the four seasons

The scene is pure f(wall_clock): season from the local date (--south flips hemispheres), day/night from the local hour (day = 07–19). Nothing is stored; the stage just dresses itself — a sky object, one weather particle type, and ground dressing along the horizon. The pet keeps living its stat-driven life inside the scene, with two crossovers: it sleeps at night and wears the snow in winter.

Scene grammar: each mode is exactly three layers — sky (sun + drifting clouds / crescent moon + twinkling stars), weather (spring petals · summer fireflies at night · fall leaves · winter snow, all falling 1 cell per tick, chunky), and ground dressing (flowers · grass tufts · leaf piles + pumpkin · snow mounds + snowman). The panel border and accents take the season hue. In winter the snow accumulates on the pet's crown (topmost pixels per column); at night the pet's eyes close and the whole dashboard dims one step, per §5.3. This is the v2 "garden" hook from the design doc: in spring, each flower could be one active day from the contribution calendar — scenery that is still derived, never stored.
