gitagotchi · the goodbye — pressing q

Quitting shouldn't feel like killing a process. The pet gets a proper sendoff: it waves, a "bye!" floats up with a heart, then the alternate screen buffer wipes downward and hands you back the exact shell you started from — cursor and all. The whole thing is ~1.4s and the wave is procedural, so every species waves its own way.

the wave · frame by frame

everyone waves goodbye

The sequence: (1) idle → (2) raise a paw on the pet's outer edge → (3–4) wave it side to side twice while "bye!" and a heart float up → (5) a happy eyes-closed ^‿^ → (6) the curtain wipe: the alt-screen fills with blank rows top-down (mimicking tput rmcup) until the shell shows through. The pet is never "closed"; it waves you off and tucks the terminal back exactly as it was — smcup/rmcup with a hidden cursor, restored on trap (design doc §8.2). The wave paw is the same procedural limb as the spear grip, just raised and swinging.
