gitagotchi · reading a book — the wisdom idle

A studious pose: the pet holds an open book and turns the pages. Like every other animation it's procedural — a book prop stamped in front of the lower body, the eyes redrawn looking down at the page, and a 4-frame page-turn. Works on all 50 species; here it's the wisdom idle, the reading-room counterpart to the sick "melt" or the happy "wiggle".

gh pet — reading

the reading pose

The book is a front-layer prop (unlike the food bowl, which sits behind) — the pet's paws rest on the cover, so it reads as held. The eye row is rewritten to a downcast vv glance, and every few seconds a page turns (the right leaf lifts and flips left). Occasionally a little ✦ "idea" sparks above the head.
state hook: wisdom idle — plays during quiet moments when wisdom ≥ 60 (reviews given, language diversity). The pet that studies is the pet that reviews others' code. Elders read with their glasses on.

frame strip · page-turn cycle

every reader — same book, fifty laps

Anchor logic (same as the beard's face-finder, reused): find the eye row, drop the book two rows below the lowest body pixel at the pet's center column, sized to the body width. The downcast eyes and the paws-on-cover pixels are placed relative to that anchor, so a tall penguin and a wide whale both hold the book at a natural height. Nothing new in the sprite files — read() is one more transform over idle_1.
