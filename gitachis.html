Reading the screen: every panel is a btop-style titled box whose border
  color is its identity — the pet panel borrows the linguist color, vitals are green, activity is
  contribution-green, friends blue, feed purple. Meters are gradient cells (red→amber→green along
  the scale, value printed — color is never the only encoding). Sparklines show each stat's
  10-refresh history in a single quiet blue. The pet blinks, gills sway, the ball bounces
  (curiosity ≥ 60), hearts float (happiness > 80) — all session-local, per the invariant.

Palette — roles, not decoration

  

  
Surface & ink are GitHub-dark (#0d1117 /
  #e6edf3) — the pet lives where the data lives. Meter gradient encodes status
  redundantly with printed values; sparklines stay single-hue so the only saturated identity
  colors on screen are the pets themselves. Tier-A (no truecolor) falls back to the §6 ASCII
  sprites and ANSI-256.
